About the Province

Located in the southeastern tip of Luzon in the Bicol Region, Albay is a province defined by its resilient history and the iconic, near-perfect cone of Mayon Volcano. Originally established during the Spanish colonial era in the 16th century, the province has stood as a beacon of Bicolano strength through centuries of volcanic activity and change. Today, it is home to world-renowned attractions such as the historic Cagsawa Ruins, the stunning views of Lignon Hill, and the unique black sand beaches that line its pristine coastline.

Visitors should explore Albay to experience a vibrant culture famous for its Ibalong Festival and spicy culinary staples like Bicol Express and Sili Ice Cream. Beyond its natural wonders, the province offers a deep connection to Filipino heritage through traditional Abaca weaving and artisan pottery. Whether you are seeking the thrill of an ATV lava trail ride or the peace of a sunset at Sumlang Lake, Albay provides an immersive and unforgettable blend of natural splendor and authentic Bicolano tradition.
